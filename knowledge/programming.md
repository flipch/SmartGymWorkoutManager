# Evidence-Based Training Programming

Curated programming reference for a program-generator. All recommendations trace to the **Sources** section. Numbers are deliberately concrete so the engine can hard-code them; ranges reflect genuine inter-individual variability, not vagueness. Debated points are flagged with **[DEBATED]**.

> Scope: healthy adults training for STRENGTH, FAT LOSS, or GENERAL/health & recomposition. Not clinical/rehab guidance. Diet and total energy balance dominate fat-loss outcomes; training shapes *what* the body composition change is made of.

---

## 1. Core principles (apply to every goal)

- **Progressive overload** is the master variable: load, reps, sets, or density must trend up over time. The engine should track and increment one of these each microcycle.
- **Volume drives hypertrophy** in a dose-response fashion (~0.2–0.4% extra growth per added weekly set), with diminishing returns; ~**10–20 hard sets per muscle per week** is the responsive range for most trained lifters. [Schoenfeld 2017; Pelland 2024]
- **Load drives maximal strength.** 1RM gains are maximized by heavy, low-rep work (≥~80% 1RM, 1–5 reps). [Schoenfeld 2021]
- **Hypertrophy is load-flexible:** similar whole-muscle growth occurs across a wide loading spectrum (≥~30% 1RM) *when sets are taken close to failure*. Strength is the load-specific adaptation, not size. [Schoenfeld 2021]
- **Frequency:** ≥**2x/week per muscle** beats 1x when volume is not equated; when weekly volume IS equated, frequency has little independent effect. Practically: spread your weekly sets over ≥2 sessions per muscle. [Schoenfeld/Grgic 2016; Schoenfeld 2019]
- **Proximity to failure:** train most working sets at **0–3 reps in reserve (RIR)**. Hypertrophy tolerates 0–3 RIR; strength work is usually 1–3 RIR (RPE 7–9) to manage fatigue and technique. [Helms 2016; Zourdos 2016]

## 2. STRENGTH goal

Goal = maximize force production / 1RM on trained movements.

- **Intensity:** ~80–95%+ 1RM. Core working sets in the **1–6 rep** band. Top singles/doubles at 90–95%+ for peaking. [Schoenfeld 2021; NSCA loading chart; ACSM 2009]
- **Sets:** 3–6 working sets per main lift; total **strength-specific** volume can be lower than hypertrophy volume because intensity is high. Keep ~10–15 weekly sets on prime movers/muscles.
- **Rest:** **3–5 min** between heavy sets. Longer rest (3 min vs 1 min) produced *greater* strength AND size gains in trained men — do not short-rest heavy compounds. [Schoenfeld 2016 rest-interval RCT; ACSM 2009]
- **Tempo:** controlled eccentric, intentful/maximal concentric ("move the bar fast").
- **% based progression:** anchor working weights to a tested or estimated 1RM and progress the percentage or the e1RM over a block (e.g., wk1 80% → wk4 87–90% at matched reps). Re-test or re-estimate every 4–8 weeks.
- **Role of RPE/RIR:** for low-rep strength work, **RPE (7–9)** is preferred over fixed % because it autoregulates for daily readiness. RPE 8 ≈ 2 RIR; RPE 9 ≈ 1 RIR; RPE 10 = 0 RIR. Trained lifters predict RIR accurately at high RPE (≈±0.2 reps at RPE 9), less so at low RPE. % and RPE are comparably effective when sets/reps are matched. [Zourdos 2016; Helms 2016; Helms/Banyard RPE-vs-%1RM 2018]
- **Rep→%1RM reference (for load math):** 1 rep≈100%, 2≈95%, 3≈93%, 4≈90%, 5≈87%, 6≈85%, 8≈80%, 10≈75%, 12≈70%. (NSCA training-load chart; individual variance is real, esp. higher reps.) [NSCA]

## 3. FAT LOSS goal

Primary driver is a **sustained caloric deficit** — training's job is to **retain lean mass** (and strength) while fat is lost, plus add some energy expenditure.

- **Energy balance is primary; macros matter for composition.** Aggressiveness of the deficit scales with starting body fat. [Aragon & Schoenfeld / ISSN 2017]
- **Protein is the lever for muscle retention:** **2.3–3.1 g/kg of *fat-free mass*** for energy-restricted resistance-trained athletes — push toward the high end as the lifter gets leaner / the deficit gets steeper. (Roughly 1.6–2.4 g/kg total bodyweight for general populations.) [Aragon & Schoenfeld / ISSN 2017; Helms 2014]
- **Keep training STIMULUS high, not "toning":** retain heavy/moderate loads (≈70–85% 1RM, ~6–12 reps) and **maintain — do not slash — weekly volume**. Volume reduction in a deficit is associated with greater lean-mass loss; maintaining/keeping ≥~10 weekly sets per muscle protects muscle. **[DEBATED]** at least one RCT found high (5 sets/ex) vs moderate (3 sets/ex) volume did not differ for lean-mass retention in trained males — i.e., *maintaining* volume matters more than *adding* it. [Helms 2014; Roth 2023]
- **Why NOT just high-rep "toning"/light circuits:** light, high-rep work without intensity provides a weaker mechanical signal to preserve muscle; the body preferentially keeps tissue it is forced to use under meaningful load. "Toning" is a marketing term, not a distinct adaptation — visible definition comes from losing fat (diet) while retaining muscle (heavy lifting + protein).
- **Conditioning / cardio / "metabolic" circuit work:** useful as an *energy-expenditure and health* tool layered ON TOP of heavy resistance training — not a replacement for it. Use 1–3 conditioning sessions/wk (steady-state and/or intervals) and optionally finish lifting days with short circuits/EMOMs for extra burn. Keep conditioning from compromising recovery of the heavy lifting that preserves muscle.
- **Practical structure:** essentially run the lifter's STRENGTH or GENERAL split, hold loads, hold volume, add conditioning, let the diet create the deficit.

## 4. GENERAL / health & recomposition (default)

Best all-round default when the user has no strong preference, or wants simultaneous modest muscle gain + fat loss.

- **Intensity / reps:** mostly **6–12 reps at ~67–80% 1RM**, with some heavier work (3–6 reps) for strength and some lighter (12–20) for joints/pump/endurance.
- **Sets:** ~**10–20 weekly sets per major muscle**, scaled to experience (beginners closer to 10; advanced toward 20).
- **Rest:** **1.5–3 min** (longer on big compounds, shorter on isolation/accessory).
- **RIR:** 1–3 RIR on most sets.
- **Recomp** (gain muscle + lose fat at once) is realistic for novices, returning lifters, or higher-body-fat individuals at maintenance or a slight deficit with high protein; harder for lean, advanced trainees. [Aragon & Schoenfeld / ISSN 2017]

## 5. Periodization & progression

All three major models produce **similar long-term strength/hypertrophy when volume and intensity are equated** — choose by logistics and adherence, not magic. [Williams 2017 meta; Grgic 2017 hypertrophy meta]

- **Linear (LP):** start higher-volume/lower-intensity, progress to lower-volume/higher-intensity across weeks (e.g., 4×10 @70% → 4×4 @85%). Simple; good for novices and peaking.
- **Daily Undulating (DUP):** vary rep/intensity by session within the week (e.g., Mon strength 4×4 @85%, Wed hypertrophy 4×10 @72%, Fri power/speed 6×3 @60% fast). Good for intermediates; lets one week hit multiple qualities. Slight edge in some short (<8 wk) studies. **[DEBATED]** edge over LP is small/inconsistent. [Williams 2017; Grgic 2017]
- **Block:** sequential phases each emphasizing one quality — **accumulation** (higher volume, ~65–75%, build work capacity/size) → **intensification** (lower volume, ~80–90%, build strength) → optional **realization/peak** (very high intensity, low volume). Good for advanced/athletes with a target date.

**Progression rules the engine can apply:**
- Add load when the top of the prescribed rep range is hit at target RIR (double-progression): reps up first, then weight, then reset reps.
- Novices: progress most sessions (near-linear). Intermediates: progress weekly. Advanced: progress per block.

**Deload rules:**
- Frequency: every **4–8 weeks**, or sooner if performance stalls / RPE creeps up at the same loads / joints and sleep degrade.
- How: cut **volume ~40–60%** (fewest sets), and/or **intensity ~10–20%**, keep movement patterns, for ~3–7 days. Volume reduction is the primary lever; keep some intensity to retain skill/neural drive.
- Pattern: typical block = 3–6 accumulating weeks → 1 deload week.

## 6. Weekly splits (with frequency rationale)

Rationale throughout: hit each muscle **≥2x/week** and land each muscle in the **10–20 weekly-set** window. More days = volume spread thinner per session (better recovery per session) but more weekly time and CNS demand.

### 3x/week — Full body (each muscle ~2–3x/wk)
Lowest frequency that still hits everything ≥2x. Best volume-efficiency for beginners/busy lifters. Use an **A/B alternating** full-body so not every lift is repeated identically each session.

| Day | Focus | Muscles hit |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| Day 1 (A) | Full body — squat focus | Quads, glutes, chest (horizontal push), back (horizontal pull), core |
| Day 2 (B) | Full body — hinge focus | Hamstrings, glutes, back (vertical pull), shoulders (vertical push), arms |
| Day 3 (A or B) | Full body — alternate/rotate | Remaining/under-hit patterns; rotate A/B weekly |

Per-session target: 1 lower compound + 1 upper push + 1 upper pull + 1–2 accessories. Each muscle ends the week with ~10–15 sets across the 3 days.

### 4x/week — Upper / Lower (each region 2x/wk)
Classic, well-supported balance of frequency and recovery. Each muscle trained exactly 2x/week.

| Day | Focus | Muscles hit |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| Day 1 | Upper A (strength lean) | Chest, back, shoulders, triceps, biceps |
| Day 2 | Lower A (strength lean) | Quads, glutes, hamstrings, calves, core |
| Day 3 | Upper B (hypertrophy lean) | Chest, back, shoulders, triceps, biceps |
| Day 4 | Lower B (hypertrophy lean) | Quads, glutes, hamstrings, calves, core |

Split ~10–20 weekly sets/muscle across the two matching days (e.g., 5–8 sets chest per upper day → 10–16/wk). Pairs naturally with DUP (A = heavier/low-rep, B = lighter/higher-rep).

### 5x/week — higher frequency / specialization
More sessions = lower per-session volume, easier recovery per session, but higher weekly time + cumulative CNS load; watch total fatigue. Two solid layouts:

**Option A — PPL + Upper/Lower hybrid (PPL+UL):** each muscle ~2x/wk.

| Day | Focus | Muscles hit |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| Day 1 | Push | Chest, shoulders, triceps |
| Day 2 | Pull | Back, rear delts, biceps |
| Day 3 | Legs | Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves |
| Day 4 | Upper | Chest, back, shoulders, arms |
| Day 5 | Lower | Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves, core |

**Option B — Upper / Lower / Full hybrid:** upper & lower muscles ~2–3x/wk via the full-body day.

| Day | Focus | Muscles hit |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| Day 1 | Upper | Chest, back, shoulders, arms |
| Day 2 | Lower | Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves |
| Day 3 | Full body / weak-point | Whatever lags + main compounds |
| Day 4 | Upper | Chest, back, shoulders, arms |
| Day 5 | Lower | Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves |

**Recovery tradeoff note:** a pure "bro split" (1 muscle/day, 1x/wk frequency) is the weaker choice for natural lifters because it violates the ≥2x/wk frequency guideline unless per-session volume is very high. Prefer the hybrids above at 5 days. [Schoenfeld/Grgic 2016 frequency meta]

## 7. Warm-up, movement prep & exercise order

**Warm-up — RAMP protocol** (Jeffreys): **R**aise (5–10 min general cardio to lift HR/temp/blood flow) → **A**ctivate key muscles → **M**obilize the ranges to be used → **P**otentiate with progressively heavier ramp-up sets of the first lift. Then 2–4 ascending warm-up sets on the first compound (e.g., empty bar → 50% → 70% → 85% of work weight) before the first working set.

**Exercise order principles** (ACSM): [ACSM 2009]
1. **Most CNS-demanding / technical first:** power & Olympic-style lifts → heavy compounds, while fresh.
2. **Compound before isolation** (multi-joint before single-joint).
3. **Large muscle groups before small.**
4. **Higher-priority / weak-point work earlier** in the session.
5. **Most intense before least intense.**

The order can be deliberately broken ("pre-exhaust") for a specific hypertrophy goal, but the default generator should follow the above.

## Key numbers (machine-usable)

### Goal → prescription
| Goal | Reps | %1RM | Sets/muscle/wk | Rest | RIR/RPE |
|------|------|------|----------------|------|---------|
| Strength | 1–6 | 80–95%+ | ~10–15 (prime movers) | 3–5 min | 1–3 RIR / RPE 7–9 |
| Hypertrophy | 6–12 (range ~5–20 valid) | 67–80% (≥30% if near failure) | 10–20 | 1.5–3 min | 0–3 RIR |
| Fat loss | 6–12 (hold load) | 70–85% | maintain ≥~10 (don't cut) | 1.5–3 min | 1–3 RIR; + 1–3 conditioning sessions/wk |
| General/health | 6–12 (+ some 3–6 & 12–20) | 67–80% | 10–20 (beginners ~10, advanced ~20) | 1.5–3 min | 1–3 RIR |

### Rep ↔ %1RM (load math)
`{1:100, 2:95, 3:93, 4:90, 5:87, 6:85, 8:80, 10:75, 12:70}` (% of 1RM). RPE↔RIR: `RPE10=0RIR, RPE9=1, RPE8=2, RPE7=3`.

### Deload rules
- Trigger: every **4–8 weeks**, or earlier on stall / RPE inflation / poor recovery.
- Action: **−40–60% volume** (primary), optionally **−10–20% intensity**, keep patterns, **~3–7 days**.
- Block shape: 3–6 hard weeks → 1 deload.
